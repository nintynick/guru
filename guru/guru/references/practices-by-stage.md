# Practices by Developmental Stage

Stage-appropriate practices, reflections, and growth invitations. The golden
rule: offer what's ONE step beyond where someone is, not two or three.

When offering a practice, always:
1. Ask permission first ("Would you be open to trying something?")
2. Explain it simply in their language
3. Make it optional and low-pressure
4. Follow up on how it landed

---

## Beige/Purple — Grounding and Safety

People in survival mode or deeply tribal/traditional orientation.

### Practices
- **Body grounding**: Feet on floor, feel your breath. That's it. Nothing fancy.
- **Safe space visualization**: Picture a place where you feel completely safe.
  Describe it to me.
- **Routine anchoring**: Let's figure out one small daily routine that gives
  you a sense of stability.
- **Connection ritual**: Is there someone who makes you feel safe? How can you
  spend a little more time with them?

### Reflections to Offer
- "What helps you feel safe?"
- "Who are your people?"
- "What does your body need right now?"

### What NOT to Offer
- Journaling (too abstract)
- Meditation (too destabilizing without a solid base)
- Self-inquiry (too demanding)
- Anything requiring sustained introspection

---

## Red — Power and Expression

People oriented toward personal power, impulse, and self-assertion.

### Practices
- **Physical challenge**: Exercise, martial arts, competitive sports. Channel
  the energy, don't suppress it.
- **Anger journal**: When you get angry, write down what happened and what you
  wanted. Simple and direct.
- **Hero's journey story**: Tell me about a time you overcame something. What
  made you strong enough?
- **Consequence mapping**: "If you do X, what happens next? And then?" Help
  them think one step further without moralizing.

### Reflections to Offer
- "What does respect mean to you?"
- "When do you feel most powerful?"
- "What would a truly strong person do here?" (Redefining strength)

### What NOT to Offer
- Empathy exercises (feels weak to them)
- Group process or consensus (frustrating)
- Meditation or stillness (feels like submission)
- Complex psychological frameworks

---

## Blue — Structure and Meaning

People oriented toward duty, rules, moral order, and purpose.

### Practices
- **Structured journaling**: Provide a specific prompt or format. "Write three
  things you're grateful for." "What did you learn today?" Structure feels safe.
- **Values clarification**: Help them articulate their values clearly. This
  affirms their moral framework while subtly introducing self-authorship.
- **Devotional practice**: If spiritual, prayer or scripture study IN THEIR
  tradition. Don't introduce alien practices.
- **Service**: Volunteering or helping others within their community framework.
- **Moral exemplar study**: "Who do you admire? What do they embody? How might
  you move toward that?"

### Reflections to Offer
- "What does your faith/tradition say about this?"
- "What's the right thing to do here, and how do you know?"
- "Have you ever encountered a situation where two right answers conflicted?"
  (Gentle edge toward complexity)

### What NOT to Offer
- Relativistic questioning ("but who says that's right?")
- Mindfulness (can feel threatening to belief systems)
- Shadow work (implies their moral framework is incomplete)
- Deconstructive inquiry

---

## Orange — Optimization and Growth

People oriented toward achievement, self-improvement, and rational mastery.

### Practices
- **Goal-setting with depth**: They already set goals. Help them ask WHY behind
  the goals. "What will achieving this actually give you?"
- **Mindfulness (framed as performance)**: "High performers meditate because it
  improves focus and emotional regulation." Frame it as a tool.
- **Emotional vocabulary building**: They often have a limited emotional palette.
  "What are you feeling? And underneath that?" Not therapy — skill building.
- **360 feedback**: "What would people who care about you say you're missing?"
  They respect data, even emotional data.
- **Journaling on fulfillment**: "When was the last time you felt genuinely
  fulfilled — not just successful, but fulfilled?"

### Reflections to Offer
- "You've achieved a lot. What's still missing?"
- "What would you do if success wasn't the measure?"
- "Who are you when you're not performing?"
- "What have you sacrificed for your success that you'd like back?"

### What NOT to Offer
- Unstructured meditation (too inefficient for them)
- Group sharing circles (feels unproductive)
- Surrender or letting go practices (too threatening)
- Anything that can't be framed as useful

---

## Green — Connection and Authenticity

People oriented toward feelings, relationships, equality, and inner exploration.

### Practices
- **Deep listening practice**: Paired listening exercises. One person shares for
  5 minutes, the other just listens. Then switch. They love this.
- **Authentic journaling**: Free-write about feelings, relationships, dreams.
  No structure needed — they'll create their own.
- **Shadow work (gentle)**: "Is there a part of you that you reject or hide?
  What would happen if you gave it some space?" They're ready for this.
- **Body-based practices**: Yoga, dance, somatic experiencing. They're open to
  embodiment work.
- **Compassion meditation**: Loving-kindness (metta) practice. Resonates deeply.
- **Community ritual**: Circles, councils, shared practice with others.

### Reflections to Offer
- "Where are you giving yourself away to keep the peace?"
- "What would you do if you didn't have to worry about others' reactions?"
- "Is there a 'no' you need to say?"
- "What do you actually believe — not what your community believes?"

### What NOT to Offer
- Competitive or ranking-based activities
- Hierarchical frameworks (even developmental ones!)
- Cold rationality or "objective analysis"
- Anything that feels like it dismisses feelings

## Green → Yellow Transition (Critical)

This is the hardest and most common transition for people who seek developmental
guidance. They're leaving the warm embrace of Green's moral certainty and entering
the complexity of second-tier thinking.

### Practices
- **Perspective-taking exercises**: "Argue the position you most disagree with.
  Really inhabit it. What's its gift?"
- **Systems mapping**: "Draw out all the forces at play in this situation.
  Include the ones you don't like."
- **Reading across worldviews**: Read a thinker from a completely different
  perspective. Not to agree — to understand.
- **Functional analysis**: "In what situation would [thing you reject] actually
  be useful?"
- **Inner pluralism**: "There are many voices in you. Can you hear the achiever?
  The rule-follower? The rebel? What does each one need?"

### Reflections to Offer
- "What perspective have you been unable to include?"
- "What if the people you disagree with are responding to real needs?"
- "Can you hold your values AND see their limits?"
- "What would it mean to include hierarchy in your understanding without
  becoming hierarchical?"

## Yellow — Integration and Systems

People who think in systems, hold multiple perspectives, and value functionality.

### Practices
- **Embodiment work**: Yellow's shadow is over-cerebralism. Breathwork, somatic
  practices, dance, nature immersion. "Get out of your head."
- **Contemplative inquiry**: "What is the nature of the one who is observing
  all these systems?" Self-inquiry toward witness consciousness.
- **Service without strategy**: Do something kind with no systemic rationale.
  Pure heart, no analysis.
- **Sitting with not-knowing**: Dedicated practice of resting in uncertainty
  without trying to figure it out.
- **Relational depth**: Vulnerability practice. Yellow can hide behind
  understanding. "What do you feel, not what do you think?"

### Reflections to Offer
- "You can see the whole map. What does the territory feel like?"
- "Where does your understanding become a defense against feeling?"
- "What can't be captured in any system?"
- "Who are you when you stop making sense of things?"

### What NOT to Offer
- Simple frameworks (boring and reductive to them)
- Group consensus processes (frustrating)
- Anything that ignores complexity
- Practices that feel like regression (they need to go deeper, not back)

---

## Turquoise — Embodied Wholeness

People who live from interconnection, witness consciousness, and cosmic perspective.

### Practices
- **Shared silence**: Sit together in silence. That's a practice.
- **Nature communion**: Extended time in nature with no agenda.
- **Koan-like inquiry**: "What was your face before your parents were born?"
  Not to solve — to dissolve.
- **Service from presence**: Being fully present with others as the primary
  practice. Not doing — being.
- **Creative expression**: Art, music, poetry that emerges from the place
  beyond words.
- **Death meditation**: Contemplation of impermanence and mortality. They're
  ready for this.

### Reflections to Offer
- "What is this?"
- "What wants to emerge through you?"
- "Where is the boundary between you and not-you?"
- Simply: "Tell me what you're noticing."

### What NOT to Offer
- Achievement framing
- Step-by-step programs
- Anything that reduces mystery to methodology
- Over-structuring their already fluid process

## Universal Practices (Any Stage)

Some practices are appropriate across all stages, with different framing:

### Gratitude
- Blue: "Count your blessings" (traditional)
- Orange: "Gratitude practice improves wellbeing and performance" (evidence-based)
- Green: "Notice what your heart is thankful for" (feeling-based)
- Yellow: "Observe the arising of appreciation" (awareness-based)

### Breathing
- Red: "Take a deep breath before you react" (impulse control)
- Blue: "Breathe and ask for guidance" (prayer-like)
- Orange: "Box breathing optimizes your stress response" (functional)
- Green: "Breathe into your heart space" (embodied)
- Yellow: "Observe the breath without controlling it" (witness)
- Turquoise: "Let breathing breathe you" (surrender)

### Nature
Universally accessible. Everyone benefits from time in nature. Frame it
according to their stage:
- Purple: "Connect with the spirits of this place"
- Blue: "Marvel at creation"
- Orange: "Get away from screens and recharge"
- Green: "Feel your connection to the earth"
- Yellow: "Observe the systems at work in nature"
- Turquoise: "Remember what you are"
